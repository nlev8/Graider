FERPA Compliance

How Graider protects student privacy and supports educational compliance

Last updated: February 2026

Executive Summary

Graider is designed from the ground up to support FERPA (Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act) compliance. As a desktop application that processes data locally and strips student identifiers before any AI processing, Graider minimizes privacy risks while providing powerful grading assistance.

Key FERPA Protections

  • No student names sent to AI: Student identifiers are automatically stripped before content is sent for grading
  • Local data storage: All student work, grades, and feedback remain on your computer
  • No cloud servers: Graider does not operate servers that store educational records
  • Direct API connection: Your computer connects directly to OpenAI - no intermediary
  • You control the data: Delete local files anytime to remove all data

What is FERPA?

The Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) is a federal law that protects the privacy of student education records. It applies to all schools that receive funding from the U.S. Department of Education. Key requirements include:

  • Schools must have written consent before disclosing personally identifiable information (PII) from education records
  • Parents and eligible students have the right to access and request corrections to education records
  • Schools must maintain reasonable security measures to protect education records
  • Third-party service providers may access PII only under specific conditions (the "school official" exception)

How Graider Addresses FERPA Requirements

1. Minimizing Data Exposure

Graider's architecture is designed to minimize the exposure of student personally identifiable information (PII). This applies to all supported AI providers (OpenAI, Anthropic Claude, Google Gemini):

Data Type Stored Locally Sent to AI Provider
Student Names Yes (your files) No - Automatically stripped
Student Work Content Yes (your files) Yes - For grading analysis
Grades & Scores Yes (local storage) No
Feedback Comments Yes (local storage) Generated by AI, stored locally
Rubrics & Settings Yes (local storage) Yes - Sent with grading request

2. Student Name Stripping

Before any content is sent to OpenAI for grading, Graider automatically:

  • Removes student names from file content
  • Replaces names with generic placeholders (e.g., "Student")
  • Strips identifying metadata from documents
  • Processes filenames to remove student identifiers

This de-identification process ensures that the AI service never receives information that could identify a specific student.

3. Local-Only Architecture

Unlike cloud-based grading tools, Graider operates entirely on your computer:

  • No Graider servers: We do not operate servers that receive or store your data
  • No user accounts: No login means no data stored on our systems
  • Your API key: You connect directly to OpenAI using your own credentials
  • Local files only: Configuration, results, and history stored in your home directory

4. Data Flow Transparency

Here's exactly what happens when you grade an assignment with Graider:

  1. You select assignment files from your local computer
  2. Graider reads and parses the files locally
  3. Student names and identifiers are stripped from the content
  4. De-identified content + your rubric are sent directly from your computer to your chosen AI provider (OpenAI, Anthropic, or Google)
  5. The AI provider returns grades and feedback
  6. Graider saves results to your local computer, re-associating student names
  7. No data is ever sent to Graider's servers (we don't have any)

The "School Official" Exception

FERPA allows schools to disclose PII to "school officials" with "legitimate educational interests" without consent. For third-party services, this typically requires:

  • A written agreement specifying permitted uses
  • That the service is under direct control of the school
  • That data is used only for specified purposes
  • Appropriate security measures

Important Consideration

While Graider strips student names before sending content to OpenAI, the student work itself is sent for AI analysis. Depending on your institution's interpretation, you may need to consider whether using OpenAI falls under the school official exception or requires separate consent. We recommend consulting with your institution's FERPA compliance officer.

  • Training: API data is NOT used to train models by default

  • No PII Sent: Graider strips student names before sending

Anthropic (Claude)

  • Training: API data is NOT used to train models
  • Retention: Inputs/outputs may be retained for up to 30 days for trust & safety

  • Policies: Privacy Policy | Commercial Terms

Google (Gemini)

  • Training: Paid API data is NOT used to train models
  • Retention: Data handling varies by product tier; API data is not retained for training
  • No PII Sent: Graider strips student names before sending
  • Policies: Privacy Policy | Gemini API Terms

Consistent Protection Across All Providers

Regardless of which AI model you choose in Graider, the same privacy protections apply: student names are stripped before any content is sent, and all results are stored locally on your computer. The AI provider never receives student PII.

Best Practices for Educators

Before Using Graider

  • Review your school's acceptable use and technology policies
  • Consult with your FERPA compliance officer if you have questions
  • Understand what data is sent to OpenAI and their retention policies
  • Consider whether your institution requires parental notification for AI tool usage

While Using Graider

  • Always review AI-generated grades and feedback before distribution
  • Keep your computer secure with strong passwords and current software updates
  • Don't share your Graider installation or output files on shared/public computers
  • Use secure, encrypted storage if saving grading results long-term

Data Retention

  • Graider stores data in ~/.graider_*/ directories
  • Delete these folders to remove all Graider data from your computer
  • Follow your institution's record retention policies for grade records
  • Consider periodic cleanup of old assignment configurations and results

For IT Administrators

If you're evaluating Graider for your school or district:

  • Architecture: Desktop application, no cloud component, no data sent to Graider
  • API Connection: Direct educator device → OpenAI (HTTPS encrypted)
  • Authentication: Individual OpenAI API keys (no shared credentials)
  • PII Handling: Student names stripped before AI transmission
  • Data Residency: All educational records remain on educator's device
  • Audit Trail: Local logs only, no centralized logging

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Graider sign a Data Processing Agreement (DPA)?

Because Graider doesn't process or store student data on our systems, a traditional DPA isn't applicable. The relevant agreement would be between your institution and OpenAI. However, since student names are stripped, OpenAI receives no PII.

Can Graider be used with students under 13 (COPPA)?

Graider is a tool for educators, not for direct student use. Students don't interact with Graider or create accounts. The educator processes student work through the application.

What if a student's name appears in their essay content?

Graider strips names from metadata and file structure. If a student writes their name within the body of their work, that text would be sent to OpenAI. We recommend instructing students not to include their names in the body of their work, or manually reviewing for this before grading.

Is the AI grading accurate enough for official grades?

Graider is a tool to assist educators, not replace their judgment. You should always review AI-generated grades and feedback before making them official. The final grading decision is yours.
